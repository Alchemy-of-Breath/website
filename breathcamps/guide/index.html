01 · Welcome

You are about to arrive somewhere quiet.

BreathCamp is a 7-day, 6-night residential retreat at ASHA, on 37 acres of untouched land in the Tuscan hills. The week is built around conscious breathing, but it is also built around food grown nearby, slow mornings, and the company of people who came looking for the same things you did.

This guide is meant to make the practical side easy, so the rest of you can arrive ready. Skim it now, and read it again the week before you travel.

04 · Getting there

How to get to ASHA.

Our address ASHA, Località Le Piane, Castell'Azzara, GR 58034
WhatsApp: +39 353 452 7348
ASHA — Alchemy School of Healing Arts

We highly recommend renting a car if you are arriving by plane (or sharing a car with others). It's easier — and most cost-effective. Once your booking is confirmed, post on the group WhatsApp to coordinate rides; most attendees travel together.

Car hire option: doyouitaly.com

By car

Put this in your navigator: shorturl.at/GMS79

By train

The nearest station is Chiusi-Chianciano Terme, about 1 hour from ASHA. A taxi from the station costs around €100+.

  • Book train tickets online (website or app): trenitalia.com
  • Cheap transport / ride-share: BlaBlaCar
  • Bus to Castell'Azzara: Rome2Rio (Chiusi → Castell'Azzara)

By plane

From Rome — Fiumicino Airport (FCO)
On the Trenitalia website select — Departure: FIUMICINO AEROPORTO, Arrival: CHIUSI-CHIANCIANO TERME. Journey is about 2h 45min with 1 change.

From Pisa — Pisa Airport (PSA)
On the Trenitalia website select — Departure: PISA FERMATA AEROPORTO, Arrival: CHIUSI-CHIANCIANO TERME. Journey is between 3h and 4h, with 2 or 3 changes.

From Florence — Florence Airport (FLR)
First, take a shuttle bus from the airport to Santa Maria Novella station (about 20 minutes; they run every hour) operated by Busitalia. Tickets at the airport or via getbybus.com.
Then on the Trenitalia website select — Departure: FIRENZE S.M. NOVELLA, Arrival: CHIUSI-CHIANCIANO TERME. Journey is between 1.5–2h. You can find a direct train or change once.

Coordinating ridesA WhatsApp group is shared with all attendees a few weeks before camp. Most people coordinate trains, taxis, and shared transfers there.

05 · What to bring

Pack light, pack soft.

Practice

  • Your own yoga mat (we recommend bringing one for safety)
  • Comfortable, layered clothes for movement and breathwork
  • A blanket or shawl you like to be wrapped in
  • An eye mask for indoor sessions
  • A journal and pen if you write

Land & weather

  • Walking shoes for the hills and trails
  • Swimwear for the pool and the nearby hot springs
  • Layers — Tuscan evenings can be cool even in summer
  • A reusable water bottle
  • Sunscreen and a sun hat
  • An umbrella or light rain jacket, just in case

If you are camping or glamping

  • A warm sleeping bag (nights can be cool)
  • A torch or head-torch
  • Anything you'd usually bring to a campsite
Weather noteWeather in May and September is variable and difficult to predict. If you've chosen tent or glamping, please come prepared. Refunds are not available for weather-related changes.

06 · Daily rhythm

Spacious, but with shape.

  • Breakfast
  • Breathwork or workshop

  • Lunch
  • Breathwork or workshop

07 · Food & dietary

Plant-based, local, generous.

Three plant-based meals a day, prepared on site with ingredients sourced locally where possible. The kitchen team can accommodate dietary needs — gluten-free, allergies, sensitivities — if you let us know in advance.

Coffee, tea, and water are available throughout the day. Snacks are kept simple and seasonal.

Let us know earlyPlease share dietary requirements at the time of booking, or as soon as you can after, so the kitchen has time to plan well.

08 · The land & nearby

37 acres, and the world around it.

ASHA sits on 37 acres of Tuscan land — stone buildings, olive groves, vineyards, walking paths. On site you'll find the swimming pool, meditation spaces, dining room, and group practice rooms including the open-air Prana Shala.

If you have a free afternoon and want to wander, nearby you'll find:

  • Natural hot springs (a short drive)
  • Etruscan historical sites in the surrounding hills
  • Pitigliano, a striking medieval town carved into volcanic rock
  • Small villages with shops, cafés, and the slower pace of rural Italy

09 · Health & safety

A few things to think about.

BreathCamp is a residential breathwork retreat, not a substitute for medical treatment, therapy, or clinical care. If you have a medical condition, please consult your doctor before attending and let us know in advance.

We strongly recommend travel insurance that covers medical care, trip interruption, and any variable circumstances that may arise.

Any updates to testing or health requirements will be communicated closer to the date as needed.

If you are pregnant, recovering from surgery, or managing a serious conditionPlease contact us before booking so we can talk through whether this week is right for you, or what adjustments may be helpful.

10 · Code of conduct

How we hold the space.

BreathCamp is a shared container. We ask everyone to bring their full selves and to look after one another's experience. The basics:

  • Confidentiality — what is shared in the circle stays in the circle
  • Respect for the land, the team, and each other's pace
  • No recreational drugs on site; no alcohol during practice times
  • Phones away during sessions and shared meals
  • Arrive on time for sessions; let us know if you need to step out

All students are required to stay on-site at ASHA during the retreat. The only exception is for resident students who live around ASHA for at least 6 months out of the year.
