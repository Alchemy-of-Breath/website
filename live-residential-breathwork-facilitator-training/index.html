A note from Anthony

For most of my early life I lived inside a story I had not written. The breath is what gave me a pen. Before I was twenty, I had found my way to conscious breathing, and I have spent the more than fifty years since following where it leads.

I have watched this work meet people in boardrooms and classrooms and in the hardest hours of their lives. And I have watched a particular kind of person arrive at our door: someone who has read the books, taken the courses, sat in the circles, and felt a quiet certainty that they are meant to offer this to others. If that is you, I want you to know the feeling is not a mistake.

Some of this work asks for a room, a fire, a circle breathing the same air. That is what the residential is for. Twenty-one days at ASHA, with our trainers beside you, learning this the only way it can truly be learned: by living it. Come and breathe with us.

About Alchemy of Breath

Breathwork taught with integrity and care.

Alchemy of Breath is the first online breathwork school in the world, founded by Anthony Abbagnano. For more than a decade, we have guided well over a million people toward the place inside where clarity, connection, and peace already live. Our practice combines effective breathwork patterns from traditions around the world into one trauma-informed system.

We are triple accredited by the Federation of Holistic Therapists, the Global Professional Breathwork Alliance, and the American Association of Drugless Practitioners, and a proud member of the International Breathwork Foundation. More than 2,000 certified facilitators have trained with us and now hold this work in their own communities.

Travel and getting to ASHA
Which airport should I fly into?+

Most participants fly into Rome Fiumicino (FCO). From there you can reach ASHA by car, train, or a pre-arranged taxi. Full travel notes are sent once you are enrolled.

Should I rent a car or take the train?+

Renting a car is the most flexible option and makes it easy to reach the nearby towns and hot springs on rest time. If you prefer not to drive, take the train toward Chiusi-Chianciano Terme, the nearest station, and arrange onward transport from there. Many participants share rides through the group chat you join after enrolling.

How do I get from the train station to ASHA?+

From Chiusi-Chianciano Terme you can arrange a local taxi to ASHA. Il Postalino is the local taxi service many of our participants use. We will share the booking details and current guidance in your travel notes once you are signed up.

Do you help coordinate travel between participants?+

Yes. Once you enroll, you join a group chat where participants coordinate shared rides from Rome and from the train station, so arrivals are easier and you meet a few faces before you even reach the land.
